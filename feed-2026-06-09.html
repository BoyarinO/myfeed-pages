State of Agent Engineering 2026

LangChain · survey of 1,300+ practitioners · 2026

Largest practitioner survey on agents in production: 57% of respondents have agents live, 70%+ use graph structures (DAG or state machine) rather than simple chains, quality is the #1 barrier (32%), and 89% have added observability — but only 52% run offline evals. Fine-tuning is reserved for niche high-impact cases; most teams still prompt-engineer on base models.

Why you'll like it: This is the empirical picture of what production agents actually look like in mid-2026 — the architecture patterns, the failure modes, the evaluation gaps. More useful than any single opinion piece for understanding where real teams get stuck.

The Trick That Offloads Intrusive Thoughts So Your Brain Can Actually Work

Big Think · evergreen

The Zeigarnik effect: unfinished tasks stay open in your working memory, pulling cognitive bandwidth until the loop is closed. The piece explains how writing down the next step — not completing the task, just externalizing the "open loop" — frees up attention for what's in front of you. A surprisingly small trick with a clear mechanism.
