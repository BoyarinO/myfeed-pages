Thoughtful Psychology & Self-Reflection

The Dalí Trick: The Strange Psychology of Becoming Who You Want to Be

Big Think · May 26, 2026

Sociologist Erving Goffman's argument: all public life is performance, not just for actors — we are all constantly playing versions of ourselves. The piece uses Dalí's deliberate persona adoption (including referring to himself in the third person) as an example of consciously choosing what to perform until it becomes genuine. Supporting evidence: the "Batman Effect" — children given an alter ego in a frustrating task persisted 22% longer. The mechanism is psychological distance from the self-as-subject.

Why you'll like it: Not "fake it till you make it" but a more interesting claim: the line between "authentic self" and "performed self" may be less real than you assume. A useful frame when you're building toward a version of yourself you haven't arrived at yet.
